# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Three mobile-first roles use the system during a one-night sports camp. Administrators manage the event, scorers work quickly under pressure and must see only their own assigned Game and Tournament slots, and team members tap an NFC tag to read only their own team balance and transaction history. The expected event size is eight teams and roughly eighteen scorers.

## Product Purpose

Provide a reliable, auditable scoring and Kaizen wallet system for a single-day camp. Administrators manage Teams, Scorers, Slots, spending, and NFC cards in separate operational areas. Game slots award win, draw, and loss scores; Tournament slots award first, second, third, and a shared score for every other participant. Every slot owns its configurable score rules and bonus cap. Success means fast mobile operation, no duplicate scoring or deductions, strict role isolation, and an understandable ledger for every team.

## Brand Personality

Energetic, focused, and trustworthy. The product should feel athletic and fast without becoming noisy or game-like. User-facing language is Egyptian Arabic in a clear RTL interface. The Saint Paul Sports Team logo is the primary brand asset.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, neon gaming interfaces, decorative glass effects, cluttered card grids, and playful interactions that slow down scoring. Do not expose shared scorer tables or make permissions appear to depend on hidden navigation alone.

## Design Principles

1. Put the next operational action first.
2. Make role boundaries visible and enforce them in the database.
3. Design for fast, low-error use at night on a phone.
4. Keep every coin movement auditable and reversible.
5. Use athletic energy through hierarchy and feedback, not decoration.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Maintain strong contrast for night use, support reduced motion, provide clear focus states and touch targets, and never communicate win, draw, loss, error, or success using color alone.
